Identifying Differentially Expressed Cluster Biomarkers

This section introduces the fundamentals of differential expression analysis in single-cell data. After clustering, the next essential step is to identify the “marker genes” that define each cluster. Ideally, a useful marker should combine both high sensitivity and high specificity. Use the cards below to explore common comparison strategies and the major functions used in Seurat.

➚ Differential Expression Strategies

One vs. All

Compare one target cluster against all remaining cells. The goal is to identify features that are relatively specific to that cluster, which is especially useful when defining broad cell types.

One vs. One

Compare two specific clusters directly, for example Cluster A versus Cluster B. This is especially useful when trying to distinguish closely related subtypes and identify subtle molecular differences.

⚙ Core Tools in Seurat

In practice, Seurat remains one of the most commonly used toolkits for cluster marker identification in R.

  • FindMarkers()

    Used to compare manually specified groups of cells, typically between two selected populations or conditions.

  • FindAllMarkers()

    Automatically iterates through all clusters and compares each one against the rest. This is the most common starting point for initial cell-type annotation.

Statistical Testing Methods

scRNA-seq data are highly sparse and affected by dropout. Choosing an appropriate test.use model is therefore important for robust differential expression analysis. Click the options below to explore the main statistical approaches.

Wilcoxon Rank Sum Test (Default)

Strengths

A nonparametric test that makes few assumptions about the data distribution, runs quickly, and performs well in many benchmarking studies.

Typical use case

A strong general-purpose default and usually the most stable place to begin.

Result Interpretation and Visualization

Once the differential expression table has been generated, the real challenge becomes biological interpretation. This section explains the key output columns and summarizes common validation plots and best-practice recommendations.

✔ Interpreting the Result Table

  • p_val_adj

    The adjusted p-value, often corrected by Bonferroni or FDR procedures. Values below 0.05 are typically considered statistically significant.

  • avg_log2FC

    The average log2 fold change in expression. Positive values indicate higher expression in the target cluster, and larger values generally indicate stronger specificity.

  • pct.1 / pct.2

    The fraction of cells expressing the gene in the target group (pct.1) and the comparison group (pct.2). An ideal marker shows high pct.1 and low pct.2.

★ Best-Practice Recommendations

1. Do Not Over-rely on p-values

Single-cell datasets often contain very large numbers of cells, so even minor differences can yield extremely significant p-values. Always interpret p-values together with log2 fold change and expression prevalence.

2. Inspect Detection Fractions Carefully

A strong marker is typically detected in a large fraction of the target cluster, for example >70%, while remaining rare in other groups, for example <20%.

3. Integrate Prior Biological Knowledge

Bioinformatics provides a shortlist, not the final answer. Final annotation should be cross-checked against resources such as CellMarker, PanglaoDB, or the literature to ensure biological plausibility.
